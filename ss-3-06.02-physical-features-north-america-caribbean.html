Learn the Story
📖
Reading the Land
The big physical features of the region

If you flew over North America and the Caribbean, you wouldn't see country borders from the sky — but you would see the shape of the land. Curators call these shapes physical features. There are four big kinds in this region.

🏔️ Mountains are the tallest land. Two long mountain ranges shape this region. The Rocky Mountains run down the western side, all the way from Canada through the United States and into Mexico. The Appalachian Mountains are older and lower, and they run down the eastern side of Canada and the United States.

🌊 Rivers carry water across the land. The Mississippi River is the longest in the region — it cuts right through the middle of the United States. The Rio Grande forms part of the border between the United States and Mexico.

🌾 Plains are wide, flat lands. The Great Plains stretch across the middle of the United States and into Canada. The land here is mostly flat with grass and farms — very different from the mountains.

🏝️ Islands are land surrounded by water. The Caribbean is mostly made of islands — big ones like Cuba and Hispaniola, and smaller ones like Jamaica and Puerto Rico.

🤔

Pause & Think: The Rocky Mountains and the Great Plains sit right next to each other. How do you think the land feels different if you're walking in the mountains versus walking across the plains?

🔎 Discovery Fact: The Mississippi River is over 2,300 miles long — water that starts as a small stream in northern Minnesota travels all the way to the Gulf of Mexico, passing through ten states on the way.
⏳

Then & Now: In the past, the Mississippi River was the main highway for moving people and goods through the middle of the continent — boats carried everything from food to mail. Today, we mostly use trucks, trains, and planes. What stayed the same? Rivers still water the farms on the Great Plains and shape the land just like they always did.

AThe Appalachian Mountains
BThe Rocky Mountains

2. What kind of physical feature is the Great Plains?
AA mountain range
BA group of islands
CA wide, flat area of land
Yes! The Great Plains is a huge area of flat grassland in the middle of the U.S. and into Canada.
The word "plains" is a clue. Plains are not mountains and they aren't surrounded by water. What kind of land are they?

AIt shows what the land actually looks like — mountains, plains, rivers, and islands.
BIt shows the names of every city in the region.
CIt tells you which language people speak.
Exactly! Political maps show borders. Physical maps show the actual land — mountains, plains, rivers, and islands.
Think about what we studied today. A physical map uses colors to show one specific thing. What does it show?
