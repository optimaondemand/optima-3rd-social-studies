Learn the Story

Why Are There Different Kinds of Maps?
Each map type answers a different question

Imagine a visitor walks into the Great Map Room and asks, "Where are the tallest mountains?" You wouldn't hand them a map of country borders — that wouldn't help at all! You'd need a map that shows how high the land is.

That's why different types of maps exist. Each one is built to answer a different kind of question. A good curator knows which map to grab depending on what the visitor needs to find out.

Today you'll meet four types of maps. Each one shows the same places — but in completely different ways.

🤔

Pause & Think: If a visitor asked, "Which country is bigger — Brazil or France?" — would you reach for a map of rivers and mountains, or a map of country borders? Why?

Investigate the Evidence
🗺️
Map Type Flip Cards
Tap each card to learn what this kind of map shows

Each drawer in the cabinet should hold one type of map. Flip each card to learn what it shows and when a curator would choose it.

🏔️
Physical Map
A physical map shows the natural features of the land — mountains, rivers, lakes, deserts, and oceans. Use it when you need to know what the land looks like. Colors usually go from green (low) to brown (high).
🏳️
Political Map
A political map shows borders between countries, states, or cities. It uses bright colors to help you tell one place from another. Use it when you need to know where one country ends and another begins.
⛰️
Elevation Map
An elevation map shows how high or low the land is above sea level. It uses layers of color — green at sea level, brown and white at the highest peaks. Use it when you need to find the tallest mountains or lowest valleys.
👥
Population Map
A population map shows where people live. Darker colors or bigger dots mean more people. Lighter areas mean fewer people. Use it when you need to know which places are crowded and which are empty.
