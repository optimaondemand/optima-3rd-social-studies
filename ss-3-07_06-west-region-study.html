Where Is the West?
📖
Land, Water, and Weather of the West
A walk through the biggest region before you map it

The West is the biggest region in the United States. It includes the whole left side of the country plus two states that don't even touch the rest: Alaska way up north and Hawaii way out in the Pacific Ocean. The other West states are California, Oregon, Washington, Idaho, Montana, Wyoming, Nevada, Utah, and Colorado.

🏔️ The land. The tall, snowy Rocky Mountains run north to south through the heart of the West. Closer to the Pacific are the Sierra Nevada and the Cascade Range — and the Cascades have active volcanoes! Other features include the Mojave Desert in California, the Great Salt Lake in Utah, and the giant redwood forests on the California coast. Alaska has icy tundra and huge glaciers. Hawaii has active volcanoes and tropical beaches.

🌊 The water. The Pacific Ocean stretches along the entire west coast. The Colorado River carved the Grand Canyon and flows through the West before reaching the Southwest. Alaska is surrounded by water — the Pacific to the south, the Arctic Ocean to the north.

🌤️ The weather. The West has the most different weather of any region. Mountain peaks have snow all year. The Mojave Desert can hit 120°F. Seattle and Portland get gentle rain almost daily. Alaska winters are long and dark. Hawaii is warm and tropical year-round.

🦅 The people and the work. The West is home to many groups: Native American nations including the Tlingit, Apache, and Hopi; Native Hawaiians whose ancestors arrived more than a thousand years ago; descendants of Chinese workers who helped build the railroads in the 1860s; Mexican-American families across California and the Southwest border; and many newer arrivals. Big cities include Los Angeles, San Francisco, Seattle, and Denver. Work includes growing fruit and almonds in California, fishing in Alaska, making movies in Hollywood, building computers in Silicon Valley, and welcoming visitors to Yellowstone and Yosemite National Parks.

The Big Picture
🏷️ Exhibit Label

The West is the biggest and most varied region of the United States. It stretches from the Rocky Mountains to the Pacific Ocean — plus Alaska's glaciers and Hawaii's volcanic islands. From redwood forests to deserts, snowy peaks to tropical beaches, the West is shaped by its mountains, its coast, and the many peoples who call it home.
