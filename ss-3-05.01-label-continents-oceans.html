Learn the Story
📖
The World's Biggest Pieces
Continents and oceans — the starting labels for any world map

If you looked at Earth from space, you'd see two things right away: large areas of land and large areas of water. The big land areas are called continents, and the big water areas are called oceans.

There are seven continents: North America, South America, Europe, Asia, Africa, Australia, and Antarctica. Some are huge — Asia is the biggest. Some are much smaller — Australia is the smallest continent.

There are five oceans: the Pacific, the Atlantic, the Indian, the Arctic, and the Southern Ocean. The Pacific Ocean is so big that all seven continents could fit inside it!

A young museum volunteer tried to put the labels up last night but got confused about which ocean was which. He mixed up the Atlantic and the Pacific. That's exactly why labels need to go in the right places — visitors trust that the map is correct.

🤔

Pause & Think: If a visitor looked at the giant map with no labels, could they tell the difference between Asia and Africa just by shape? What would help them know for sure?
