Eight Directions on the Compass Rose
Cardinal and intermediate directions

Last time, you learned that a compass rose shows directions on a map. You already know the four cardinal directions: north, south, east, and west. These are the main ones.

But what if something isn't exactly north or exactly east? What if it's in between? That's where the four intermediate directions come in: northeast (NE), northwest (NW), southeast (SE), and southwest (SW). They fill the gaps between the cardinal directions.

Think of it like a clock: north is at the top (12), east is on the right (3), south is at the bottom (6), and west is on the left (9). The intermediate directions sit between them — northeast is between 12 and 3, and so on.

🤔

Pause & Think: If you're facing north and turn halfway toward east, which direction are you facing now?

🔎 Discovery Fact
The word "compass" comes from an old word meaning "to step together." Sailors used compasses to step their way across the ocean, direction by direction!
