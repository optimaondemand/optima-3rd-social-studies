What Makes a Strong Evidence Entry
The three parts the museum director looks for.

The museum director explained that every evidence entry needs three things:

1. Observation: What did you notice in the source? Be specific — name details, colors, objects, people, dates, or places.

2. Meaning: What does the source tell us? Connect the details to a bigger idea — what was life like? What was happening?

3. Importance: Why does this source matter? What would we not understand without it? Why should visitors care?

A strong entry is honest (it reports what you actually see), specific (it names real details), and clear (a visitor can understand it).
