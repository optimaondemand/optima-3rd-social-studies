The Four Fields of Social Studies
Every field asks different questions about our world.

Social studies is the study of people, places, and communities — past and present. It's how we learn about the world and our role in it. The museum needs curators who understand all four fields.

📜 History is the study of the past. Historians ask: What happened? Why did it happen? What can we learn from it? They study old letters, photographs, maps, and objects to find answers.

🗺️ Geography is the study of places. Geographers study maps, mountains, rivers, oceans, and how people live in different parts of the world. They ask: Where is it? What is it like there?

💰 Economics is the study of how people use resources. Economists ask: What do people need? How do they get it? Why do people trade? If you've ever bought something at a store or traded a snack with a friend, you've done economics!

🏛️ Civics is the study of citizenship and government. Good citizens follow rules, help their neighbors, and work together. Civics asks: What are the rules? Who makes them? How can citizens make a difference?
