Five Panels, One Story
How the Exploration Wing fits together

Step back and look at what you've built. The Exploration Wing tells one big story, and it takes five panels to tell it honestly.

Motives — why people explored. Curiosity, trade, land, and religion pushed people to leave home. That's where the story starts.

Tools — how they navigated. Compasses pointed north. Astrolabes measured the stars. Portolan charts showed coastlines. Caravels could sail into the wind. Without these tools, no voyage would have worked.

Routes — where they went. Columbus sailed west to the Caribbean. Magellan's crew circled the globe. Ponce de León reached Florida. Each route is a line on a map — but each one tells a real story.

Discoveries — what they found. New lands, new peoples, new plants and animals. The Columbian Exchange moved foods and animals across the Atlantic in both directions.

Consequences — what happened next. Disease killed many native people. Explorers took land. People were forced to work. The world became connected — but the cost was high. An honest exhibit doesn't hide this.

🤔

Pause & Think: If you removed any one of these five panels, what would be missing from the story? Which panel do you think visitors need the most?

Quick Self-Check
🧠

Last self-check of the unit. These questions pull from everything you've learned across all six lessons.

1. Which of these is a motive for exploration — a reason people explored?
AA compass that points north.
BA caravel that can sail into the wind.
CThe desire to find faster trade routes to Asia.
Right! The desire for trade routes is a motive — a reason people explored. The compass and caravel are tools, not motives.
A motive is a reason someone does something. Which of these answers describes a reason, not a tool?
2. Which category does the Columbian Exchange belong in?
ATools — because it helped explorers navigate.
BConsequences — because it happened after explorers arrived.
CMotives — because it inspired people to explore.
Yes! The Columbian Exchange was a consequence — something that happened after exploration connected the continents.
The Columbian Exchange happened after explorers reached the Americas. Which category fits something that happened as a result?
3. Why does the museum director say an honest exhibit includes consequences?
ABecause the full story includes what happened to people and places, not just the discoveries.
BBecause consequences make the exhibit look longer and more impressive.
CBecause museums are only allowed to show sad things.
Exactly. Honesty means showing the whole picture — the exciting discoveries and the real effects on people and places.
Think about what honesty means in a museum. Would a visitor trust an exhibit that hid important parts of the story?
