Learn the Story

What Are Sources?
How curators sort the evidence before writing labels.

Last time, you learned that historians study the past. But how do they know what happened? They use sources — objects, documents, and images that give us information about the past. The evidence tray on your table has two kinds.

⭐ Primary sources were created during the time they tell about. They're firsthand clues from the past. A diary, an old photograph, a letter, a tool, or a coin — these are all primary sources because they were made by people who were actually there.

📖 Secondary sources were created after the time they tell about. They're someone else's description or explanation. A textbook, an encyclopedia article, or a documentary about history — these are secondary sources because they were made later by people who studied the past.

🤔

Pause & Think: If you found a 100-year-old letter in your grandmother's attic, would that be a primary or secondary source? Why?

🏷️ Exhibit Label

Primary sources are firsthand clues from the past — made during the time they describe. Secondary sources are created later by people who studied the past. Honest curators study primary sources carefully before writing any exhibit label.
