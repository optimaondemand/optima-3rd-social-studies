Learn the Two Sides
📖
Costs, Benefits, and the Wise Decision Between Them
Every marketplace choice has two sides

Back in the very first lesson of this unit, you learned that every choice has something given up. Today, the curator wants you to see those two sides clearly.

A cost is what you give up or what you put in when you make a choice. Costs are not always money. A cost can be time, effort, space, or even another opportunity. If you spend an hour playing one game, the time you can't spend on another game is a cost. If a town builds a harbor, the money, the trees cut down, and the years of work are all costs.

A benefit is what you gain when you make a choice. Benefits can be new goods, new services, saved time, more comfort, more income, or more fun. If a town builds a harbor, the new jobs, the goods that can arrive by ship, and the new travelers are all benefits.

A wise decision means looking at both sides before you choose. If the benefits clearly outweigh the costs, the choice usually makes sense. If the costs clearly outweigh the benefits, it usually doesn't. Sometimes the two are close, and a person has to use their best judgment. That careful thinking is what the curator calls self-governance — and it's what good marketplace decisions are built on.

The Decision Board — "Should the Town Build a New Harbor?"
A worked example, side by side

Here's the question the head curator wants to show visitors. Read the tiles on each side carefully.

➖ Costs (what we give up) ➕ Benefits (what we gain)
Money to build the docks and storehouses. New jobs building, fishing, and unloading ships.
Trees from the forest used for wood. More goods arrive from far away — sugar, fish, citrus.
Years of work before the harbor is ready. Travelers visit and spend money in town.
Loud ships, more traffic in town. Local crafters can ship their goods to other places.
🤔

Pause & Think: Look at both columns. Which side has more weight — the costs, or the benefits? Is there one tile on either side that matters more than the rest? What would you decide if you were on the town council?

🤔

Pause & Think: Think about a real choice you made this week. What did you gain (a benefit)? What did you give up (a cost)? Did you weigh both sides before you chose, or did you grab the shiny thing first?

⏳

Then & Now: Long ago, town councils met for weeks to weigh costs and benefits of new roads, harbors, or markets. Today, cities still hold meetings and make charts of costs and benefits before big decisions — they just use computers and slideshows instead of chalkboards. What stayed the same about the need to weigh both sides?
