🏛️ Virtue Spotlight — Courtesy
Today you'll compare two regions — the Northeast and the Southeast. Courtesy means we don't say one region is "better" than the other. They're different in many ways, and both are home to real people. We notice the differences with kind, careful words.

Where Is the Southeast?
📖
Land, Water, and Weather of the Southeast
A walk through the region before you map it

The Southeast sits in the lower-right corner of the United States. It touches two big bodies of water — the Atlantic Ocean on the east and the Gulf of Mexico on the south. Twelve states make up this region, including Florida, Georgia, Alabama, Mississippi, Louisiana, South Carolina, North Carolina, Tennessee, Kentucky, Virginia, West Virginia, and Arkansas.

🏔️ The land. Remember the Appalachian Mountains from the Northeast? They keep going! The southern end of that same mountain chain runs through Tennessee, North Carolina, and Georgia. East and south of the mountains, the land becomes flat — this is called the coastal plain. Florida's southern tip is mostly swamp and wetland, called the Everglades.

🌊 The water. The mighty Mississippi River flows down through Louisiana and empties into the Gulf of Mexico. The Atlantic gives the Southeast long, sandy beaches. Cities like Charleston, Miami, and New Orleans grew up near water because trade and fishing were easy.

☀️ The weather. Winters here are mild — sometimes warm enough for shorts! Summers are hot and humid. The Southeast also has a hurricane season from June to November, when big storms can blow in off the Atlantic or the Gulf.

🌴 The people and the work. Farms in the Southeast grow many things you may have eaten — oranges and grapefruit in Florida, peaches in Georgia, peanuts in Alabama, cotton across many states. New Orleans is famous for jazz music. The Southeast is also where important moments in the Civil Rights Movement happened.

The Big Picture
🏷️ Exhibit Label

The Southeast is the lower-right region of the United States, touching both the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico. It has warm weather, the southern Appalachians, the mighty Mississippi River, and the Everglades. From orange groves in Florida to jazz in New Orleans, the Southeast is shaped by its warm coasts, its long rivers, and the people who call it home.

Not graded — just for you! Answer these three questions before writing your Map Quest response.

1. Which TWO bodies of water touch the Southeast region?
AThe Pacific Ocean and the Atlantic Ocean
BThe Great Lakes and the Pacific Ocean
CThe Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico
Right! The Southeast touches the Atlantic on the east and the Gulf of Mexico on the south. That's why it has so many beaches.
Look at your U.S. map. The Southeast is in the lower-right corner. Which two waters border it?
2. Which big river flows through the Southeast and empties into the Gulf of Mexico?
AThe Hudson River
BThe Mississippi River
CThe Delaware River
Yes! The Mississippi River is one of the longest rivers in North America. It flows south and empties into the Gulf at Louisiana.
The Hudson and Delaware are Northeast rivers. Think of the big river we said flows down through Louisiana.
3. How is the Southeast's weather different from the Northeast's?
AThe Southeast has milder winters and hot, humid summers — plus hurricane season.
BThe Southeast is colder than the Northeast all year.
CThe Southeast has snow most of the year.
Exactly! The Southeast is generally warmer and more humid than the Northeast, and it can get hurricanes off the Atlantic or Gulf.
Remember — the Southeast sits further south, closer to the equator. That makes it warmer than the Northeast, not colder.
