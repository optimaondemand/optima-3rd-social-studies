Learn the Story

What Word Roots Tell Us
How roots unlock meaning

Here's the thing about geographic terms — they aren't random. People didn't just make up the word "peninsula" because it sounded nice. They put two Latin pieces together: paene (almost) + insula (island). The word itself is a description.

The same is true for many terms on our World Map. When you see terrain, the root terra (land) is right there. When you hear aquatic, you can hear aqua (water). The roots are clues hiding inside the words.

This matters for curators because when you write a label for the map, you can use roots to explain terms clearly. The volunteer in the World Map Hall was stuck on the word archipelago. He couldn't remember what it meant. But when the head curator told him the Greek root means "chief sea," it clicked — an archipelago is a chain of islands in a sea. The root told the story.

🤔

🔎 Discovery Fact
The word "Arctic" comes from the Greek word arktos, meaning "bear" — because the northern sky has the constellation Ursa Major, the Great Bear!

Then & Now: Ancient Romans and Greeks named the places they discovered using their own languages. Thousands of years later, we still use those roots. The word Mediterranean literally means "middle of the land." Some names never change.

Quick Self-Check
🧠

Not graded — just for you! Answer these questions to see how much you remember about word roots.

1. The root geo- means "earth." What does a geographer study?
AThe ocean floor
BThe earth and its features
COuter space
That's right! Geo- means earth, so a geographer studies the earth and its features.
Think about the root — geo- means "earth." What would studying the earth include?
2. The root terra- means "land." Which word most likely describes the surface of the land?
ATerrain
BAquarium
CCompass
Yes! Terrain contains the root terra- (land) — it describes what the surface of the land looks like.
Look for the word that has terra- hiding inside it.
3. If pen- means "almost" and insula means "island," what is a peninsula?
AA small island far from shore
BLand surrounded by water on three sides
CA deep ocean trench
Exactly! A peninsula is "almost" an island — land with water on three sides, but still connected to the mainland.
"Almost an island" means the land is surrounded by water on most sides but still connected somewhere.
4. The root aqua- means "water." An aqueduct is a structure that ___.
AGrows crops on dry land
BCarries water from one place to another
CMeasures temperature
Right! An aqueduct carries water — the aqua- root is your clue.
The root aqua- means water. What would a structure with "water" in its name do?
