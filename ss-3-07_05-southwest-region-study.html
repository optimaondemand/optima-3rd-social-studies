Where Is the Southwest?
📖
Land, Water, and Weather of the Southwest
A walk through the region before you map it

The Southwest sits in the lower-middle part of the United States. The four main states are Texas, Oklahoma, New Mexico, and Arizona. The southern edge of the Southwest borders the country of Mexico. Texas is so big it stretches all the way to the Gulf of Mexico, but most of the Southwest is far from any ocean.

🏜️ The land. Much of the Southwest is desert — dry land with little rain. The Sonoran Desert covers parts of Arizona, and the Chihuahuan Desert spreads across New Mexico and Texas. The region has tall flat-topped hills called mesas and buttes. Arizona is home to the famous Grand Canyon, which is over one mile deep and 277 miles long.

🌊 The water. Water is precious in the Southwest because it doesn't rain often. The most important river is the Rio Grande, which forms part of the border between Texas and Mexico. The Colorado River carved out the Grand Canyon over millions of years. People here build dams and canals to save and share water.

☀️ The weather. Summers in the desert are very hot — sometimes over 100°F. Winters are usually mild and sunny. Rain is rare, but when it does rain hard, water can rush down dry creek beds in flash floods.

🌵 The people and the work. The Southwest has been home to Pueblo, Navajo, Hopi, and other Native American nations for thousands of years. Many Pueblo families still live in adobe villages made of mud bricks. The Southwest is also home to many Mexican-American families whose families have lived in this region for generations. Work in the region includes cattle ranching in Texas, oil and gas drilling, growing cotton, and welcoming visitors to the Grand Canyon and other parks.

The Big Picture
🏷️ Exhibit Label

The Southwest is a region of deserts, canyons, mesas, and big skies. Its four main states — Texas, Oklahoma, New Mexico, and Arizona — share hot, dry weather and a long border with Mexico. From the Grand Canyon to Pueblo villages and Mexican-American communities, the Southwest is shaped by its dry land, its precious water, and the many peoples who have called it home for thousands of years.

🦉
🧭 Opti says…
Did you know that some saguaro cactuses in the Southwest are older than the United States? Native peoples have lived alongside those cactuses, drawn maps of the canyons, and named the mesas long before any of our other regions had cities. The Southwest holds some of the oldest stories on the whole continent.

Canada is north of the United States. Think about which country is to the south of Texas, New Mexico, and Arizona.
